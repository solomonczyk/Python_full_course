# First Batch Invitation Messages

This document contains pre-prepared messages for the operator to use when inviting, explaining, and following up with the first batch of 5–10 beta participants.

**Tone guidelines:** honest, no pressure, no "finished product" claim, no "mass launch" claim, no promise of full course access without feedback.

## Short Warm Invite

> Hey [Name]!
>
> I've been working on a little thing — an interactive story where kids learn real Python by playing through a space adventure, helping characters on a distant space station.
>
> I'm looking for a few families to try it out for free before I open it wider. Would your [son/daughter] be interested? They just need a laptop and about 30 minutes to try Part 1.
>
> Happy to explain more if you're curious!

## Parent Explanation (Detailed)

> Here's a bit more detail about Python Quest:
>
> **What it is:**
> - A story-driven Python course for beginners (~10–13 years old)
> - Kids write real Python code to solve problems and progress through an adventure
> - No block coding, no drag-and-drop — real code from the start
> - Self-paced, browser-based, no installation needed
>
> **What it is NOT:**
> - ❌ Not a finished product — it's a beta with rough edges
> - ❌ Not a complete course yet — only Part 1 is fully ready
> - ❌ Not a replacement for school or tutoring
>
> **During the beta, you get:**
> - Free access to the course
> - A unique beta code for your family
> - Part 1 access immediately
> - Parts 2–5 unlocked gradually after your feedback
>
> **What I need from you:**
> - Let your child try Part 1 (~30 minutes)
> - Share honest feedback afterwards (5-minute form)
> - Let me know if something breaks

## Staged Access Explanation

> Python Quest has 5 parts, but only Part 1 is open when you start.
>
> **Part 1** — opens immediately with your beta code (starts with lesson 1-1)
> **Parts 2–5** — locked until you share feedback and the operator unlocks them
>
> Why staged access?
>
> 1. **Focus** — your child concentrates on one part at a time
> 2. **Quality** — I catch and fix issues before more content is open
> 3. **Your voice matters** — your feedback shapes what gets improved before the next part
> 4. **Safety** — if something breaks, it only affects one part, not the whole course
>
> The unlock process:
> 1. Your child completes Part 1 (takes ~30 minutes)
> 2. You fill out the feedback form (5 minutes)
> 3. The operator reviews your feedback (usually within 24–48 hours)
> 4. If everything looks good, Part 2 is unlocked
> 5. You receive a confirmation message
>
> ⚠️ Part 2 unlock is **not automatic**. It requires your feedback and operator review. This is intentional — it keeps the experience safe and focused.

## Privacy Explanation

> **What I do NOT collect from your child:**
> - ❌ Full name
> - ❌ Email address
> - ❌ Phone number
> - ❌ Precise age or birthdate
> - ❌ Address or school
> - ❌ Photos, audio, or video
>
> **What the course stores technically:**
> - A beta code (random identifier like `BETA-K7M3A9`)
> - Lesson progress (which lessons were completed)
> - Mission results (code output for programming exercises)
> - All stored in a database, linked only to the beta code, not to your child
>
> **What I ask from you (manually, outside the product):**
> - Your contact info — only what you choose to share (email, Telegram, etc.)
> - Honest feedback about the course
>
> **No child accounts.** The course does not require registration, login, or any personal information from your child. The beta code is all that's needed.
>
> If you have specific privacy questions, I'm happy to answer them.

## Feedback Requirement Explanation

> After your child completes Part 1, I need your feedback before I can unlock Part 2. This is a key part of the beta:
>
> **Why feedback is required:**
> - Your honest input helps me improve the course
> - I need to know what works and what doesn't
> - If something is broken, I need to fix it before more content opens
>
> **The feedback form asks:**
> - How did it go overall? (rating 1–5)
> - What did your child enjoy?
> - Where did they get stuck?
> - Would they like to continue?
> - Any suggestions or issues?
>
> **What happens after you submit:**
> 1. The operator reviews your feedback (usually within 24–48 hours)
> 2. If no critical blockers, Part 2 is unlocked
> 3. You get a notification
> 4. Your child continues to Part 2

## What to Do If Something Breaks

> If something doesn't work:
>
> 1. **Refresh the page** — most issues resolve with a refresh
> 2. **Try a different browser** — latest Chrome or Firefox recommended
> 3. **Take a screenshot** (if possible) and send it to me
> 4. **Describe the problem** — what were you doing, what happened, what did you expect?
> 5. **Message me directly** — I'll help as soon as I can
>
> Please tell me about any issues right away. If something is broken for you, it's likely broken for others too, and fixing it early helps everyone.
>
> Common quick fixes:
> - "Nothing happens when I click Start" → refresh the page, or try a different browser
> - "The code checker says I'm wrong" → check for typos, capital letters, and punctuation
> - "A page looks wrong" → take a screenshot and send it to me
> - "The site won't load" → check your internet connection, try again after a minute

> 4. **Beta safety** — It's easier to manage a small beta when content is staged.
>
> The unlock process is simple: finish Part 1 → share feedback → I review → Part 2 opens. It usually happens within 24–48 hours.
