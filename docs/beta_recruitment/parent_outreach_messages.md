## Message Explaining Staged Access

> Great question! Here's why access opens one part at a time:
>
> Python Quest has 3 parts, and Part 2 and Part 3 are locked by default. When your child finishes Part 1, I ask you for honest feedback — what worked, what was confusing, what could be better.
>
> Once I receive your feedback, I review it and unlock Part 2.
>
> Why do it this way?
> - I want to catch issues early, before too much content is open
> - Your feedback directly shapes what gets improved
> - It keeps the experience focused — one part at a time
> - If something breaks, it only affects one part, not the whole course
>
> It's not a permanent lock — just a way to make sure the course actually works for real kids before opening everything.

## Message If Parent Asks Why Full Course Is Locked

> I understand it might seem unusual! Here's why I'm doing staged access:
>
> 1. **Quality control** — I want to make sure Part 1 works perfectly before opening Part 2. If something breaks, it only affects one part.
> 2. **Feedback-driven improvement** — Your feedback after Part 1 helps me fix issues before your child sees Part 2.
> 3. **Pacing** — Young beginners learn better when content is focused. Completing Part 1 first gives a sense of achievement before moving on.
> 4. **Beta safety** — It's easier to manage a small beta when content is staged. This keeps the experience stable.
>
> The unlock process is simple: finish Part 1 → share feedback → I review → Part 2 opens. It usually happens within 24–48 hours.
