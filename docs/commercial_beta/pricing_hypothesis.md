# Python Quest Pricing Hypothesis

## Pricing Goal

Validate willingness to pay for a story-driven, mission-based Python course for beginners — without pretending the product is final, complete, or mass-market ready.

The beta price is not intended to maximise revenue. It is intended to:
1. Confirm that parents perceive enough value to pay.
2. Filter for genuinely interested, engaged beta participants.
3. Establish a price anchor for the eventual mass-market launch.
4. Generate initial revenue data to validate the business model.

---

## Recommended Beta Price

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Price** | $14.99 |
| **Currency** | USD |
| **Access duration** | 12 weeks from beta code activation |
| **Renewal** | Not automated. Manual extension if beta period is not concluded. |
| **Payment method** | Manual (invitation link with Stripe payment link or similar). Not automated inside the product. |

### Reason for $14.99
- **Below impulse-buy threshold:** $14.99 is low enough that parents do not expect a perfect, finished product — consistent with the "controlled beta" framing.
- **Above free:** A non-zero price filters for engaged participants who are genuinely interested in learning Python, not just clicking through.
- **Comparable to a children's book or app:** $14.99 is what a parent might spend on a workbook, a puzzle app, or a single tutoring session — making it an easy yes.
- **Anchor for future pricing:** The eventual mass-market price ($29–$49) will feel justified relative to the beta price when more features (parent dashboard, full account system, certificates) are added.
- **Refund expectations are manageable:** At this price point, refund requests are unlikely to be a burden even with manual processing.

### Why Not Free
- Free beta attracted 58 participants and confirmed product-market fit. A paid beta tests willingness to pay, which free access cannot validate.
- A non-zero price ensures that participants have skin in the game — feedback from paying users is higher quality.
- Free access can always be offered again later (scholarships, referral programs, school pilots).

### Why Not Higher ($29+)
- At $29+, parents expect a finished product with parent dashboard, certificates, and support. The beta cannot deliver these yet.
- Higher price would require automated payment, refund handling, and legal terms — which are explicitly out of scope for this layer.

## Alternative Price Tests

| Variant | Price | Access | Purpose |
|---------|-------|--------|---------|
| **Low beta price** | $9.99 | 6 weeks | Test price sensitivity. If $14.99 converts poorly, test whether $9.99 changes behaviour significantly. |
| **Standard beta price** | $14.99 | 12 weeks | Primary hypothesis. Recommended for first commercial beta cohort. |
| **Family/friend test price** | $4.99 | 12 weeks | Special price for referrals, word-of-mouth testers, or multi-child families. Tests whether social proof (referral) compensates for lower price. |

### Price Test Strategy
1. Start with the **standard beta price** ($14.99) for the first cohort (10–20 participants).
2. If conversion rate from landing → purchase is below 30%, test the **low beta price** ($9.99) with the next cohort.
3. Run the **family/friend test price** only as a controlled referral experiment with existing beta participants.

## Success Criteria

| Criterion | Measurement | Target |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Parent understands value | Post-purchase survey question: "Do you feel the price is fair for what you received?" | ≥70% say yes |
| Parent accepts beta limitations | Post-purchase survey question: "Were the beta limitations clearly communicated and acceptable?" | ≥80% say yes |
| Payment interest is confirmed | At least 20 paid beta participants | ≥20 purchases |
| Refund/support expectations are clear | Refund rate | <10% (ideally 0%) |
| Conversion from free to paid | % of contacted free beta participants who convert when offered the paid beta | ≥25% |
| Price anchor established | Post-beta willingness-to-pay survey for full version | ≥$29 median |

---

## Pricing Privacy Note

- No payment data is stored inside the Python Quest application.
- Payment processing happens externally (Stripe payment link or manual invoice).
- The beta code is delivered after payment confirmation — the purchase event and the learning environment are fully separated.
