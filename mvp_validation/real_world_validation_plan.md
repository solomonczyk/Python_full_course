# Real World Validation Plan: Python Quest MVP
**Status**: READY_FOR_REAL_WORLD_TEST  
**Duration**: 14 days  
**Goal**: Validate market demand with minimal resources

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This plan executes real-world market validation for Python Quest using prepared materials:
- Landing page copy (3 offer variants)
- 3 demo lessons (print/input, if/else, lists)
- Community posting kit (5 short + 3 long posts)
- Tracking system for metrics

**No platform building. No payments. No auth.**

---

## PHASE 1: SETUP (Days 1-2)

### Day 1: Landing Page
**Goal**: Deploy simple landing with email capture

**Actions**:
1. Create Carrd.co or Tilda page (free tier)
2. Copy: Use `landing_mvp_copy.md` — Offer 1 as primary
3. Form: Email + "Who are you?" (3 options) + "Python experience?" (3 options)
4. Confirmation: "Check your email for Lesson 1"
5. Connect: Email service (Mailchimp free tier, or Telegram bot)

**Time**: 3-4 hours  
**Cost**: €0

**Success checkpoint**:
- [ ] Page loads on mobile
- [ ] Form captures email
- [ ] Confirmation sends
- [ ] Analytics track page views

---

### Day 2: Demo Lessons Delivery
**Goal**: Prepare 3 lessons for email delivery

**Actions**:
1. Format Lesson 1 from `demo_lessons_selection.md` as email
2. Format Lesson 2
3. Format Lesson 3
4. Set up drip sequence:
   - Day 0: Lesson 1 (immediately after signup)
   - Day 1: Lesson 2
   - Day 2: Lesson 3 + survey
5. Test all emails render correctly

**Time**: 3-4 hours  
**Cost**: €0

**Email subject lines**:
- Lesson 1: "Урок 1: Твой голос в коде (Python Quest)"
- Lesson 2: "Урок 2: Выбор пути — if и else"
- Lesson 3: "Урок 3: Инвентарь героя + финальный проект"

**Survey questions (end of Lesson 3)**:
1. Что понравилось больше всего?
2. Что было непонятно?
3. Стал бы ты проходить полный курс? (1-5)
4. Что для тебя важнее: результат / процесс / поддержка / другое
5. Если бы курс стоил €29-49 — купил бы? (да/нет/не уверен)
6. Свободный комментарий

## PHASE 3: ITERATION (Days 8-10)

### Day 8: Analyze & Adjust
**Goal**: Understand what's working

**Actions**:
1. Review tracking spreadsheet
2. Identify:
   - Best performing offer variant
   - Most engaged segment
   - Top objections
   - Price sensitivity signals
3. Decide: continue current offer or pivot positioning

**Decision matrix**:
| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Offer 1 > Offer 3 in engagement | Continue gaming angle |
| Offer 3 > Offer 1 in emails | Switch to adult positioning |
| Parents most engaged | Double down on parent communities |
| Dropouts most engaged | Focus on "struggled before" messaging |
| "Too childish" > 20% | Pivot to adult variant immediately |
| No clear winner | Run A/B test on landing |

**Time**: 3 hours analysis + 2 hours adjustments

---

### Day 14: Decision
**Goal**: GO / SOFT GO / PIVOT / NO-GO

**Criteria**:

| Metric | GO | SOFT GO | PIVOT | NO-GO |
|--------|----|---------|-------|-------|
| Landing views | 300+ | 100-300 | 50-100 | < 50 |
| Email signups | 30+ | 10-30 | 5-10 | < 5 |
| Capture rate | > 15% | 8-15% | 5-8% | < 5% |
| Demo completes | 10+ | 5-10 | 2-5 | < 2 |
| Would pay €39 | 30%+ | 15-30% | 5-15% | < 5% |
| Price inquiries | 3+ | 1-2 | 0 | 0 |

**Decision paths**:

**GO**: All criteria met or exceeded
- Proceed to early access sales (€19)
- Build simple payment page
- Deliver content via email/PDF/Telegram
- Use revenue to fund platform

**SOFT GO**: Mixed signals but traction
- Extend validation for 1 more week
- Double down on best performing segment
- Refine offer based on feedback
- Consider early access with limited scope

**PIVOT**: Weak but some signals
- Change positioning (adult vs teen)
- Change format (remove characters?)
- Change target segment
- Run 1 more week with new hypothesis

**NO-GO**: No market signal
- Archive project
- Open-source content for community
- Document learnings
- Move to next idea

## SUCCESS SCENARIOS

### Scenario A: Strong Teen/Parent Response
**Signals**: 50%+ signups from teen/parent segment, "where for my kid?" questions
**Action**: Double down on Offer 1, build parent-focused materials, consider parent-child bundle pricing

### Scenario B: Strong Dropout Response
**Signals**: "This is what I needed" comments, high completion rate, emotional engagement
**Action**: Emphasize Offer 2, add testimonials, create "finished when others quit" messaging

### Scenario C: Strong Adult Response (Surprise)
**Signals**: 40%+ adult signups, professional questions, "no fluff" appreciation
**Action**: Pivot to Offer 3, create professional landing variant, downplay "game" word

### Scenario D: Weak Overall
**Signals**: < 100 views, < 5 emails, "nice idea but..." responses
**Action**: NO-GO; document and move on
